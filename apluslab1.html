Using CLI (cmd.exe) commands to find out information about our systems.


Recommendation:

Change your command line settings: font, font color, layout, so as to feel more comfortable. Just right click on the left corner and go to properties.



Exercise 1:   Basic commands. Type them.

winver

systeminfo

dxdiag

msinfo32


Exercise 2:   WMIC (Windows Management Instrumentation Console) commands. Type them, trying to make sense of what you typed.

wmic baseboard list brief /format:list

wmic bios list brief /format:list

wmic computersystem list brief /format:list

wmic cpu list brief /format:list

wmic memorychip list brief /format:list

wmic diskdrive list brief /format:list

wmic desktopmonitor list brief /format:list

wmic logicaldisk list brief /format:list

wmic memphysical list brief /format:list

wmic nic list brief /format:list

wmic os list brief /format:list

wmic pagefile list brief /format:list

wmic recoveros list brief /format:list

wmic systemslot list brief /format:list

wmic irq list brief /format:list ----note: devmgmt.msc is better for this.

wmic port list brief /format:list ----note: devmgmt.msc is better for this.

wmic /? ---note: for your reference.
